Design Docs:

         Evidence of care and ownership:

o    Custom icon/logo, developer names

         Completeness

o    Descriptions: Game World, Uniqueness,

o    Feature comparisons with similar games

o    Sketches: E.g., UI Sketch,

o    Samples arts, style

         Working doc:

o    Cross out instead of delete (so you know what you have done and won't forget)

         WATCH OUT: for feature creep!!

         Why are important:

o    Comparisons with other games! Why is yours special.

o    Game World:

  Show ARTS!! Convey a sense of what things will look like

o    Game objects:

  List out EXACTLY how many objects your game will have, what each object looks like

  Know how many (different objects) you will be working with

o    Features:

  When you have many features/characteristics (e.g., speed, jump, armor, ), you MUST

         list all of these out, and derive a table or graph to relate these visually,

         you will have to spend time tuning these in relation to each other, you need to know their relationships explicitly!

o    User Interface

  What this looks like, figure that out NOW!

o    Audio:

  Don't get overly ambitious

  List out _EXACTLY_ how many you need, and when each will be invoked.

   

o    Un-needed fields:

  Delete them to make the document more compact

  Do NOT delete things you may need later (e.g., not sure about particle system? Write "not sure" for now)

o    Length of game play (realistic for our class):

  5 to 10 minutes

         Examples:

o    2014: KillAWatt

o    2013: MusicTD, Haxxit, JailBreak

o    Why I like these (may include):

  Include/use own icons, logo for team (shows ownership)! [OK to customize!!]

  Include sample arts

  Include versions of the document

  Feature comparison with similar games
