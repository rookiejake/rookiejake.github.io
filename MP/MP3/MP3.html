The World and the main camera

The world will now consist of four cameras.

        Please fix your game display resolution (to workaround UI elements moving as window size change), mine is set at 1600x900. You can see my solution not working well by doing a full window option on my MP3 solution. The UI text not aligned properly is ok for MP3.

        MainCamera

o   Size = 100 (height of the world window would be 200-units)

o   Viewport, anchored at the lower left corner, occupies 80% of both width and 100% of height of the display area

        You must continue to support the cool down bar, prominently showing the cool down in between updates.

        Note,

o   All Unity cameras must be set to Orthogonal (and not Perspective)

o   The camera position must have a z-value of -10 (otherwise, your objects may not be inside the view volume).

o   Also, make sure the scale is set to (1, 1, 1), and rotation is set to (0, 0, 0)

Waypoints and Waypoint Cam

        Waypoints

o   Upon collision with Eggs, they shake at a frequency of 10 cycles per second, for a varied duration and magnitude

o   First hit shake duration of 1 second, magnitude of 1x1

o   Second hit shake duration of 2 seconds, magnitude of 2x2

o   Third hit shake duration of 3 seconds, magnitude of 3x3

        Waypoint Cam

o   Viewport location is aligned with lower-right of the display area, width is 20% and height is 30% of the display area.

o   Camera world height is 8 units size is 8 units, or world window height is 16 units

o   When camera first come alive, its position will center on the target waypoint

o   Initially, this is disabled (not showing anything) [Hint: gameObject.SetActive(false)]

o   Life time

  Waypoint cam will be enabled and focused immediately on the first waypoint that begins the shaking process

  Waypoint cam will be disabled again when the shaking process is done

o   When two waypoints are shaking simultaneously (shoot at one, and before it is done shaking, shoot another one), the Waypoint Cam only response to the first one that went into Shaking and its operation is completely unrelated to the second shaking waypoint.

o   Label: there must be a label clearly indicating this cam and its state (active or disabled)

Enemy and Enemy Cam

        Enemy

o   All enemies are always spawned within the view of the MainCamera. My implementation spawns enemy randomly within 80% of the height and width of the MainCamera view.

o   For testing purposes

  Traveling speed: MUCH slower (for testing purposes), at 5 units/second

  State durations: set both size change and rotation duration to be 25 frames (instead of the 120 and 80 from MP2).

  There can only be exactly 5 enemies in the scene

o   Hit response:

  Upon collision with eggs, enemy is pushed and Lerp in the direction of the egg (transform.up)

  Transition into Stunned State: Pushed by 4 units settling in 2 seconds at a rate of 2 (for the TimedLerp rate)

        Position = Position + (Size * Direction)

  Transition into Egg State: Push by 8 units, again, settling in about 2 seconds at a rate of 2

        Enemy Cam

o   Viewport location is located right on top of the WayPoint Cam, width is 20% and height is 30% of the display area.

o   Camera world height is a variable (refer to the following)

o   Initially, this is disabled (not showing anything)

o   Life time

  Enemy cam will be enabled and focused immediately on the first chasing enemy and the hero

  Enemy cam will be disabled when the enemy transition out of the Chase state (either via cool off, collision with the hero, OR, bring hit by an egg).

  When alive,

        camera height should be set to the distance between the chasing enemy and hero

o   Size = (heroPosition enemyPosition).magnitude

        Camera position should be the middle between the chasing enemy and the hero

o   Position = (heroPosition + enemyPosition) * 0.5f

o   Check bug: Put an enemy into ChaseState and then shoot it into Stunned State, is EnemyCam properly disabled?

o   Label: there must be a label clearly indicating this cam and its state (active or disabled)

Hero Cam

The hero cam must always be active and always focusing on the hero.

  • Viewport location is right on top of the enemy cam, as others, width and height is 20%x30% of display width and height
  • Hero cam lerps after the mouse movement, with a duration of 0.5 seconds (quick!), and TimeLerp rate of 8
  • When eggs are spawn, hero cam goes into shake with default camera shaking parameters and magnitude of 1x1
  • Hero cam size is 8 (with world window height of 16 units)

Hints

        Begin working with Hero Cam first, it is the most straightforward, then waypoint cam, and last on enemy cam.

        You will have to modify the utilities. I my solution, I have to modify all of CameraSupport, TimedLerp, and ShapePosition to support what is described. This is by design, you need to receive code given to you, owe it, and modify it to suit what you need.

        The given hints on how to compute positions and directions are all based on simple Vector math. ASK ME if you dont understand, and, be inspired and go take a course on linear algebra.

        When a camera is shut off, dont worry if it is stuck with the last frame shown and not showing black.

o   Greg has a simple and smart workaround for this problem. Simply define another camera with an overlapping viewport with world window slight behind the corresponding camera. In this way, when you enable the camera in the front (e.g., the enemy cam) it will block the camera viewport of the back camera, and when the front camera is disable, the back-camera will show through. If you dont understand this, no worries.
